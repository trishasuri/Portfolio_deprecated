Sailor Moon Adventure

Action/Adventure Web Game

Object-Oriented Programming for RPG model

Background

During my time at Facebook and Instagram, I took an Introduction to Python class where I had my first taste of programming. I learned to setup a virtual server, fork a repo, create branches, and work with other engineers approve and land my diffs (which in this case were my Python homework assignments, lol). I was hooked and at the end of the course, the main goal was to build a game that would take user input to go through a series of scenes. I wanted to take what I had learned at Facebook and apply it to a web game with animated scenes.

My Role

I programmed the game and designed the architecture behind it.

Process

I had a solid skeleton for the architecture of the game. I then picked up tricks online to code the game and animate it. I also took a Javascript course on Codeacademy to learn more about Object Oriented Programming, and also learned that I would need to know PyGame in order to fully create something I was satisfied with.

Outcome

In the span of a month, I saw a tremedous spike in sales and had to play product pushes by ear. I would A/B test different phone case designs on the Etsy platform to see which would perform better. I ran a couple ads which didn't seem to affect my sales too much. I also monitoried analytics, search engine traffic, keywords, seo, etc. Eventually, with my newly gathered knowledge, I would like to expand the business by creating a website and competing head to head with companies like Casetify, who have a really high markup on their cases because they are able to print the cases in-house. Ideally, I would like to buy a 3D sublimation printer to cut my manufacturing costs too.

Methods and Tools

Etsy, Adobe Photoshop, Excel, Google Sheets, Paper Prototype, Cost-Profit-Analysis, SEO, online marketing, business anlytics
