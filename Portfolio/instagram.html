Background

The Gratitude Journal is a happiness tracker and emotionally intelligent app that can also be paired with a wearable device. It's designed to help you record gratefulness data, send you positivity throughout your day, and encourage you to share your happiness with the world around you. Each day you add 5 things you are either grateful for or look forward to in the day. This allows you to create a routine by feeling gratitude on a daily basis.

This project was created for Mermaid Labs LLC, a developer group creating mobile and wearable apps with an emphasis on free, open source products.

My role

I designed the mobile application's UX and UI, and built the high-fidelity prototype.

Process

The original idea for this project came about when I discovered the gratitude apps on the Apple store were priced between .99-$4.99. I was frustrated to find that developers were monetizing mental health solutions so I wanted to publish a similar, but improved app for free. I wanted to keep the main elements of tracking gratitude the same, but I wanted to include a few new features that these apps didn't have. Specifically, I wanted to make the app compatible with the Apple Watch, by generating arbitrary positivity notifications throughout the day.

Once I had my vision, I created a prototype of the app using Sketch 3 and did two informal usability tests. This led to a few changes to the overall flow of the app.

We concluded by running three informal usability tests to ensure the prototype was successful.

Outcome

The project is a work in progress. I have applied to an iOS bootcamp to learn how to publish the app without relying on outside help and I'm also learning how to develop for Facebook Independent apps.

I'm continuosly exploring both options while working full time at Instagram and applying to graduate programs.
